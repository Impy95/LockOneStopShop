Tier

Our tier 20 is the Diabolic Raiment.

  • 2 piece: Gives your Corruption a 0.65 RPPM chance to proc a soulshard. This doesn't scale with number of targets, because it's RPPM.
  • 4 piece: Gives your shard spenders a 25% chance to proc a 15% haste buff for 8 seconds.

Our tier 19 is the Legacy of Azj'Aqir.

  • 2 piece: Gives you 10% more Unstable Affliction damage.
  • 4 piece: Gives your Agony a 10% higher chance to generate a shard.

Our T20 2PC was heavily nerfed after release, but is still more shards generated than our old T19 4PC. Our T20 4PC is incredibly powerful and should be acquired ASAP.

The old T19 2PC is worth a decent amount, usually enough to use worse legendaries (prydaz/belt/ring) over better ones like the helm. This will heavily depend on the item level of your T19 gear, so I recommend simming yourself.

Conclusions

  • Affliction legendaries are pretty well balanced, so which you use will depend heavily on your raid composition and the individual fight. In general, Helm, Belt, and Chest are of similar value and your best choices for raw dps. I recommend not using chest if possible because it's extremely RNG for affliction. The belt also pulls ahead on cleave, as more of your UAs can be cast on targets without UAs.
  • Soul of the Netherlord is slightly lower overall dps than the legendaries above, but gives you INCREDIBLY powerful execute. There are multiple fights this is great on (desolate host has a 35% burn). It's also always a good legendary if you think your guild will have people die, making execute last longer.
  • Sacrolashs Dark Strike is BiS on a few fights where you have many targets out for long periods of time in conjunction with Absolute Corruption. Namely Demonic Inquisition and Desolate Host. The slow is also incredibly useful on multiple fights.
  • Prydaz, Xavaric's Magnum Opus is only a few percent (1-2% depending on gear) behind the better legendaries, and provides a large increase to survivability. It's always a solid choice for progression, particularly on fights that are very healing intensive.
  • Streten's Sleepless Shackles has some niche uses for cleave fights where you want to run contagion. It's not recommended for beginners because it's pretty hard to juggle agony and corruption on multiple targets and also cycle UAs. The only notable cases of this I can think of are Mythic Harjatan, where you can keep up 3+ UAs for a large part of the fight, and Fallen Avatar if you need more split damage on the add.
  • Sephuz's Secret and The Master Harvester are both very powerful in conjunction with Sow the Seeds for things like Mythic+.
